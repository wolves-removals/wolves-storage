## Temperature / humidity targets (cite freely)
- Archival/paper storage: **13–20°C, 35–60% RH** — PD5454:2012, UK National Archives. [STANDARD]
- General mixed household collection: **~18–22°C, 45–55% RH** — Canadian Conservation Institute (CCI). [STANDARD]
- Stability matters as much as the number: rapid RH cycling causes microscopic structural damage; slow seasonal drift inside the band is fine — PD5454. [FACT]

## The mould clock (the killer stat)
- Mould red line **~70% RH**: at 70% RH mould develops in roughly **3 months**; at ~90% RH in **a few days** — CCI. [FACT]
- Therefore STORAGE DURATION is decisive: a damp unit that never floods still grows mould given enough weeks above 70%.
- Stagnant air accelerates mould; ventilation + goods off floor/away from external walls is the defence — PD5454. [FACT]

## Why drive-up metal units "sweat" in a UK winter (dew-point science — unique angle #1)
- **Dew point** = the temperature at which air saturates and water condenses. Any surface at/below the dew point collects water — no leak required. [FACT]
- A metal-skinned/single-skin unit has almost no thermal mass, so its walls/roller-door track the cold and become the coldest surfaces. When mild, moisture-laden air hits the cold metal (below dew point), condensation forms on the metal first ("sweating"). [FACT]
- RH rises as temperature falls, so an unheated unit can sit above the 70% mould line for long winter spells with no visible water. [FACT]
- Countermeasures PD5454 names: gentle humidistat heat (keeps surfaces above dew point) + ventilation (removes moist air before it condenses) — what a managed ventilated warehouse has and a cold sealed drive-up box lacks. [STANDARD]

## Per-item preparation
- **Wood/veneer**: hygroscopic — warps/cracks on RH swings, veneer lifts. Clean, dry, stable 40–55% RH. [FACT]
- **Leather**: ideal stable 45–55% RH, 18–22°C; dry heated air can hit ~5% RH → cracking/stiffening; high RH → mould — CCI. [FACT]
- **Fabrics/upholstery**: avoid damp >70% RH (mould); launder+dry (residues attract moths/beetles); acid-free tissue. [FACT]
- **Documents/photos**: 13–20°C, 35–60% RH; damp → foxing/mould; acid-free boxes buffer swings — PD5454. [STANDARD]
- **Electronics/white goods**: drain+dry fully (residual water → mould/odour); let cold items acclimatise before powering on. [FACT]
- **Mattresses**: breathable/vented bag, flat, dry — not thick sealed plastic. [RULE-OF-THUMB]

## Packing science (unique angle #3)
- **NEVER seal wood/leather/mattresses/upholstery in airtight plastic** — traps moisture → mould/warp; leather can go brittle in ~3 months. Use BREATHABLE blankets/cotton. Stretch-wrap is for short transit, not long sealed storage. [FACT/RULE-OF-THUMB]
- **Acid-free (lignin-free) tissue** for textiles/paper — ordinary card/newsprint is acidic, yellows and embrittles. [STANDARD]
- Boxes act as a humidity flywheel (buffer swings) — PD5454. Keep goods off the floor / away from external walls. [STANDARD]
- Heaviest low, fragiles high; plates/glass on edge; cushion all six faces. [RULE-OF-THUMB]

## UK storage law (unique angle #5)
- Conventional drive-up self-storage = a **LICENCE to occupy, explicitly NOT bailment**; goods stored "at own risk"; operator takes "no care, custody or control". [FACT]
- Managed/packed container storage (operator collects, packs, seals, handles, stores) = the classic test for **BAILMENT** → a bailee-for-reward owes a **duty of reasonable care** and to return goods in the condition received — a genuinely higher duty. (Contract terms can modify; this is the common-law default.) [FACT — flag contract-dependent]

## Insurance (unique angle #4)
- **>90%** of UK operators make insurance a condition of storage (SSA UK). Not a legal requirement, but industry standard. [FACT]
- Typically COVERS: fire, flood, escape of water, theft with forcible/violent entry, storm, impact, riot. [FACT]
- Standard EXCLUSIONS that bite: **damp / condensation / MOULD**, gradual deterioration / wear / rust, internally-arising vermin; often cash/jewellery/furs/perishables/flammables. [FACT]
- Consumer takeaway: the single most common storage loss (mould/damp) is the one insurance usually WON'T pay → the dry environment, not the certificate, is the real risk control.

## Prohibited items — the real reasons
- Flammables/fuels: vapour can ignite even from "empty" containers (fire regs/insurers). Explosives/ammo/fireworks: illegal + fire. Perishables: spoil + attract pests. Living things/plants: can't survive + attract vermin. Unifying rationale = fire risk, legal compliance, protecting other customers' goods in a shared building. [FACT]

## Economics & sizing (for honest comparisons)
- Removals volume rule-of-thumb (label as such): 1-bed ~300–400 ft³, 2-bed ~450–600, 3-bed ~700–1,000, 4-bed ~1,000–1,400. Reliable method = itemised L×W×H. [RULE-OF-THUMB]
- A typical wooden storage vault ≈ 250 ft³ → a 3-bed house ≈ 3–5 containers (confirm Wolves' real internal volume). [RULE-OF-THUMB]
- True all-in self-storage cost ≠ headline rent: add mandatory insurance + van hire + fuel + YOUR time (two trips) + padlock + access limits. UK avg rental return ~£29.13/sq ft/yr (SSA UK 2025). [FACT]
- UK market: ~2,915 facilities, 64.3M sq ft, 75.1% occupancy; container-based ~39% and the fast-growing segment (SSA UK 2025). [FACT]
